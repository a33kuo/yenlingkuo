Thank you for visiting Yen-Ling's website!

I am a Ph.D. student in MIT CSAIL advised by Dr. Boris Katz. I'm working on planning and plan understanding with compositional machine learning models to enhance robots' capabilities in interacting with humans and physical environments.

Before coming to MIT, I was a software engineer at Google Mountain View working on visual search for shopping and new shopping ads features. I received my M.S. and B.S. in Computer Science at National Taiwan University, where I completed my master thesis on crowdsourcing and reasoning of commonsense knowledge in the Intelligent Agents Lab. I also spent a semester as a visiting student in the Software Agent Group at MIT Media Lab working on intelligent user interface. My master thesis is the best summary of my previous work.

My research interests are to build machine that can act robustly, understand humans, be understood by humans. In more specific, my current work asks questions like:

  • How do humans plan so effectively in various environments?
    How can robots plan as effectively?
  • How do humans understand dangers in an environment to adjust their plans?
    How can machines understand humans' believes of dangers?
  • How do humans communicate their plans via actions?
    How can machines recognize humans' plans and help them complete tasks?
  • How do humans memorize and compose plans using languages?
    How can machines transform the learned planning models to languages and make it programmable?
