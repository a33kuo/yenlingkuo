MovISee

2014/09 - Present

Collaboration: Yen-Ting Cho, Yen-Ting Yeh

MovISee is a digital software and platform for people to play and create individual games and videos with their own existing digital files. We use depth camera to create mixed reality for people to explore the selected information and ultimately transform people understanding of their body movement as means to composite customized visual output.

Cubic Film

2013/01 - 2014/09

Collaboration: Yen-Ting Cho, Yen-Ting Yeh

Cubic film is a system to layer film frames into pixel cube and provide different scanning mechanisms for users to experience moving images in new ways/dimensions. Each new scanning line produces a row of picture elements/pixels and disrupts the recorded time and spatial relationship.

Hand Painted Film Plus

2012/01 - 2012/09

Collaboration: Yen-Ting Cho, Yen-Ting Yeh

Hand Painted Film Plus is a digital interface to create film strip, allowing participants to paint with camera, re-configuring the created sequence to explore new patterns and experiencing the transformation between time and space. Participants were especially surprised to see how static images moved and vice versa.

Flora: Mobile Flower Image Recognition Service

2010/04 - 2011/07, Gold Award of 2010 Hinet Telsoft Competition and 8th YuShow Cup Creativity Award

Flora is an iPhone application for ordinary people to identify what a flower is. Users just take a picture of the unknown flower, the app will automatically send picture to cloud, process image, then send back all information about this flower. Flora also has a build-in flower card collection game to promote interaction with flowers. Whenever user finds a flower, s/he is awarded with flower card to share with friends.

Automatic Panoramic Image Stitching using CUDA

2010 Spring, Parallel Programming Final Project

This is an application for finding panorama automatically. We implemented SIFT feature extraction/match, gain compensation, and multi-band blending in CUDA. Using CUDA, we improve the performance of Panoramic Image Stitching nearly 10 times faster. Our system can build the panorama in one minute with 6 1000x800px input images, whereas the sequential version takes about 12 minutes to compute the same panorama.

SDIO Card with World Real Time Clock/Alarm

2007 Fall, Digital System Design Final Project

This project simulates a SDIO card interface to control and display a world real time clock on LCD. It supports three functionalities: real time clock, alarm, and change time zone. The card can operate with battery only, there is no need to connect it to a SDIO host.
